Here are some examples of site I currently have in my 'favorites' shortcut menu. Some of these I visit everyday, hopefully you'll find something interesting or humorous.

site: FOX News
url: http://www.foxnews.com
comments: This is the website for the cable network of the same name. Their reporters shoot right down the middle, but their commentators opine to the right. There is no better place for breaking news. They post headlines right off the wire, even before the story link is up.
site: Dictionary.com
url: http://www.dictionary.com
comments: Who doesn't need to look up a word occasionally? This is a great dictionary because it's available anywhere, anytime, and provides alternative definition sources. A premium membership removes the ads and offers audio pronunciations, but it isn't necessary.
site: The Washington Times
url: http://www.washingtontimes.com
comments: Not to be confused with the Washington Post, this is another conservative leaning news site. They have the best sources in the capital, just try them. Anything written by Bill Gertz is usually picked up by other reporters.
site: IGN
url: http://www.ign.com
comments: This is the best video game website ever. I've been reading them since they were just "N64.com" and now they've grown quite a bit. They have sections on every console, PC gaming, gadgets, movies and more but the Cube section is especially good. Unfortunately you must now be an 'Insider' for full access.
site: Car and Driver
url: http://www.caranddriver.com
comments: I don't read their print magazine, but the online version is great. I can't vouch for the new layout yet, but they still provide daily news from the automotive world that I like. Such timely and specific updates are impossible to find elsewhere.
site: Road & Track
url: http://www.roadandtrack.com
comments: This is the site connected to the print magazine that I read every month. The content is essentially the same in both sources and honestly the site gets it second (since it's free). But the site does have pictures not in the magazine, so it's worth checking out.
site: Home Star Runner
url: http://www.homestarrunner.com
comments: No one quite knows what this site is, but it is packed with hilarious flash movies and shorts. Despite Home Star being the title character, a guy named 'Strong Bad' is the reason to visit. His email is especially funny.
