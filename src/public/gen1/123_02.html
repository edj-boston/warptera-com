This section should be useful if you want to see how models made around the same time share characteristics or inspiration. You should be able to see how my technique and style have progressed from the very beginning.

I have also provided comments for each model that may include information about its name, inspiration, or technology. Clicking the preview image for each model opens a live constructor window so you can see it in action.

Click on the blue hypertext below to choose a range of twenty models (the links of unopened ranges will remain in order):

001-020
021 name: Q_Axis
genre: walker/motored
comments: Named in reference to Z_Axis, this model used a similar mechanism but has very different dimensions. It is similar to Ligament and submitted at the same time so I was surprised to see both models get in the zoo.
022 name: Ligament
genre: walker/motored
comments: This model's joints remind me of ligaments. It was submitted with a similar model named Q_Axis. Both use the offset timing method from Z_Axis.
023 name: Radiance
genre: walker/motored
comments: Radiance was only the second of my models to provoke acclaim from the community and even earned a kind word from our usually silent zookeeper. The 3-day construction process was tedious because I had to constantly rebuild the legs to experiment. It is a little too large but its legs radiate out nicely from the center.
024 name: Segmental
genre: snake/flex
comments: I love how this model appears to consume itself as it slithers along. At the time I was just learning to program flex structures so every result was a surprise. This was a good one.
025 name: Slithering_Bubbles
genre: snake/flex
comments: This is the same structure as Segmental programmed differently. I wanted to achieve an effect like when a mass of pressurized water shoots through a hose in a cartoon.
026 name: Nifty_Little_Guy
genre: roller
comments: This configuration resulted from experimenting with different programming for a triangle structure. It can be made into a nice linear motor combination, but this way is a little like a turbine. It is the only time I have rushed naming a model and now it seems a little out of character.
027 name: Curvsive
genre: zero-g/flex
comments: This model is the same structure as Segmental and Slithering_Bubbles with a different program. It reminds me of cursive handwriting with a calligraphy pen.
028 name: PurpleHexagon_KevinoStyle
genre: amoeba/flex
comments: This model has the dubious distinction of being the most often poached in the zoo. Surprisingly, many people like to twist it into odd shapes and resubmit it. It can even look like Kevino's original TriangleQ when tangled up, although I don't condone it.
029 name: Panzer_Amoeba
genre: amoeba/flex
comments: This model is really just a simple amoeba but it employs what I learned from Kevino's TriangleQ. The supports are programmed to distort the overall shape. I felt it looked like the design of some old German Panzer tanks.
030 name: Cold_Fusion
genre: walker/motored
comments: Coaldiamond posted a motor in the zoo that was a 3pt version of what we now call a Counter Rotor. He wondered aloud if it could be used to power a walker and Jeckyll and I both figured out ways to do it (though I prefer his way). The first part of the name is meant to recall the beginning of sound in Coaldiamond's name.
031 name: Medium_Panzer
genre: amoeba/flex
comments: When I tried applying the same technique I used in Panzer_Amoeba to a larger shape I saw that the distortion was more defined. I did a very conscientious job of tuning and was happy to see such a subtle modification get into the zoo.
032 name: Chasing_the_Wrinkle
genre: zero-g/flex
comments: Coming up upon this model I was more and more point density in my flex chains. I went a little overboard here and have backed off every since. Nevertheless, the high-speed extension and compression of this structure causes it to get ahead of itself, creating the interesting wrinkle effect.
033 name: Simplification
genre: walker
comments: When I make a series of increasingly complex models, I always burn out. Hence you will often see an exceedingly simple model such as this one following them. This is the first time it happened so it earned the obvious name. It is probably also my fastest walker and might be a decent soda racer with a little tuning.
034 name: Pontoon
genre: amoeba/flex
comments: This is essentially the same model as Medium_Panzer, but with a different program. The supports are programmed to increase with the part of the flex chain they attach to so it creating this elongated amoeba structure. I felt it looked like a pontoon used on some of the boats seen on a lake I frequent in the summers.
035 name: JellyBean
genre: amoeba/flex
comments: While Medium_Panzer had its supports out of phase with its flex chain and Pontoon's where in phase, JellyBean's are 1/4 phase apart. It created a strange shape that I tuned to look like it was partially suspended as it rolled. I submitted a zero-g version but of course it was rejected for being too similar.
036 name: Gemini_Twins
genre: zero-g/flex
comments: This is actually two separate but identical structures. I experimented with different arrangements but settled on having one perpendicular to the other. They are basically similar to Approaching_Theta but they each have two waves running their flex loops instead of one.
037 name: Organelle
genre: zero-g/flex
comments: Organelle uses three of the same structure that Gemini_Twins used. The main difference is that they have some masses in common and so intertwine when they move.
038 name: Propelled
genre: zero-g/flex
comments: Propelled is the same structure as Organelle except the loops are untangled. Unfortunately though, they both share the same mistake. One of the loops is connected one mass off. It doesn't make a whole lot of difference in terms of the finished model, but it bugs me and I wish I had caught it.
039 name: Radiate
genre: walker/motored
comments: Here I tried to revisit the same motors and basic principles at work in Radiance. The result is a little out of proportion however and much less majestic. The name indicates the lineage but is appropriately lackluster.
040 name: Schadenfreude
genre: walker/motored
comments: This is based on Radiate and is one of only two or three of my models that I just don't like. Firstly, the structure seems almost crippled. And secondly, the leg braces bend each motor out of shape. The name means 'shameful joy' in German but there is no joy here.
