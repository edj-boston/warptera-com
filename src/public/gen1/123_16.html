This section should be useful if you want to see how models made around the same time share characteristics or inspiration. You should be able to see how my technique and style have progressed from the very beginning.

I have also provided comments for each model that may include information about its name, inspiration, or technology. Clicking the preview image for each model opens a live constructor window so you can see it in action.

301 name: Minos
genre: walker/motored
comments: The technology employed by Minos is related to Crete. I named it after the ancient king of that island who used to demand the sacrifice of young Athenians.
302 name: Minotaur
genre: walker/motored
comments: The Minotaur was the monster that Minos, king of Crete, had in his labyrinth. The odd elliptical pattern produced by these cone shaped linear combinations is slightly squashed so the walker tends to sway back and forth as it moves, giving it an eerie frankenstein gait.
303 name: Theseus
genre: walker/motored
comments: Theseus was the hero who slew the Minotaur and ended the ritual sacrifices. He also appears in Sophocles' "Oedipus at Colonus," one of my favorite plays. The leg design and motor usage of both Crete and Theseus make them two of my best walkers.
304 name: Perspective
genre: walker/motored
comments: This model uses the skew programming technique to bend or warp the cone linear combinations. I named it Perspective because of the lines that meet at the center points look like a road that disappears over the horizon.
305 name: Man_of_War
genre: walker/motored
comments: Man_of_War uses the skew programming technique. It also has legs like both Crete and Theseus but they cross at the center. The result produced a surprising stable walker with a very smooth stride. I named it after the poisonous jellyfish that shares its basic shape.
306 name: Achilles
genre: walker/motored
comments: Achilles uses RAM linkages in place of Crete's and Theseus' cone linears and elliptical amoebas. But I think it doesn't look as good as those two because the motor is too large and the legs are proportioned wrong.
307 name: Zenith
genre: walker/motored
comments: This model took a lot of patience to program. The relative amplitude needed to be just right to get the rotary portion of the motors to line up correctly. I like it a lot because it has an asymmetric frame yet still produces a nice walking machine.
308 name: Curie
genre: walker
comments: Curie was meant to imitate lonelyswedish's style of intricate four-legged walker, but something went awry because it is too symmetrical and the toes recall my own Radiance model. I named it after Pierre Curie in a veiled reference to that model's radioactive name.
309 name: Waver
genre: walker/motored
comments: This model uses 2pt compound linear motors and is related in structure to Minotaur. In fact, it suffered the same swaying motion but to a much larger degree. Because of this, I tuned it to exaggerate the effect and named it for this property.
310 name: Gutenberg
genre: walker/motored
comments: This model uses 2pt compound linear motors and vaguely recalls Danube although it has fewer legs. I decided to call it Gutenberg after the man who invented the printing press, making so much learning and progress both possible and accessible.
311 name: Sway
genre: walker/motored
comments: Sway is like both Minotaur and Waver, but it uses linear actuators instead of cone linear combinations. As a result it has the same funny squashed ellipse pattern but it is reversed. In retrospect I should have lover the relative amplitude of the compound linear motor to reduce the swaying.
312 name: Rotato
genre: walker/motored
comments: I got the idea for Rotato from some of Jeckyll's models. The leg motion is actually very similar to Antler in that each set of legs swings up and over the frame before coming back down to take its turn carrying the load.
313 name: Fist
genre: walker
comments: Fist is my second attempt to mimic the style of lonelyswedish. I think it was more successful than Curie but I doubt anyone actually mistook it for one of his models. I don't know what it is but lonelyswedish is difficult to imitate.
314 name: Differential
genre: walker
comments: This is the simple model I am most proud of. Probably because it marks the invention of my phased compound motors. Using these techniques you can create compound motors where the ends are no longer mirror images of each other. It can get complex but amazing things are possible.
315 name: Triceps
genre: walker/motored
comments: Using 3pt Phased Compound Motors I was finally able to construct the model I had in mind when I built Method. It looks similar to Quadriceps so I named it in kind but the structures are actually very different.
316 name: Interconnectedness
genre: zero-g/motored
comments: Interconnectedness is my version of some 2pt linear combinations that Jeckyll started working with. It required intense guideline and programming work but the result is one of my most complex and beautiful models.
317 name: Bow_Tie
genre: walker/motored
comments: This uses a 3-sided 2pt compound linear combination that combines compound linear motors and MultiMotors. The main portion was actually excerpted from Interconnectedness to save time in construction.
318 name: Conservation
genre: other/physics
comments: This zero-g/zero-f model demonstrates the conservation of angular momentum. It took many tries to get it jump started without too much vibration. Sometimes I imagine viewing a figure skater from above doing one of those spins when I look at it.
319 name: Winch
genre: walker/motored
comments: Winch is a combination of 2pt compound motors that makes a series of 1pt RAMs with center points. I then connected a tension spring to the center point and the power point to yield another usable power point to connect the legs.
320 name: King_Crab
genre: walker/motored
comments: King_Crab uses 2pt Phased Compound Motors to drive a counter rotor linkage in the bottom. Then I hit upon the idea of using 4pt anchored linear motors to brace the tops of the 2pt ones, then I used the anchor points to mount the legs onto.
